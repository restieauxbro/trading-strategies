# 2026 Watchlist Review

Date: 2026-04-20
Universe: `/Users/tim/Downloads/strong_upward_momentum_20Apr2026_1819.csv` (90 tickers)

## Scope

This pass is intentionally chartless. I did not use TradingView or price-pattern work. The decision is based on:

- business quality and secular tailwinds
- recent operating momentum
- whether the stock still looks worth following after a big run
- near-term event risk, especially earnings in late April / May 2026

## Market Context

- `SPY` closed about 7.1% above its 200-day average and `QQQ` about 8.6% above its 200-day average on the latest available daily data I pulled locally, so the broad tape still looks constructive rather than defensive.
- Cboe's VIX was back around the low-20 area by 2026-04-17 after the March volatility spike, which supports staying selective but still leaning toward leadership rather than deep-value repair trades.
- That matters for this list: I prefer secular compounders and infrastructure names over late-cycle mean-reversion trades.

## Verdict

If I were running one watchlist for the rest of 2026 from this scan, I would concentrate on the AI infrastructure stack, data-center power/cooling, and a small number of high-quality compounders. The names I would spend the most time on are:

1. `ANET`
2. `AVGO`
3. `APH`
4. `GOOG`
5. `ETN`
6. `PWR`
7. `TT`
8. `EME`
9. `EQIX`
10. `IBKR`

## Secondary List

These are good names, but I would need either a pullback, a post-earnings reset, or better valuation support before promoting them into the core list:

- `AAPL`: High-quality mega-cap, but the upside is more measured than the core AI-infra names.
- `ADI`: Strong analog recovery candidate; I prefer it after earnings rather than into a sharp run.
- `AMD`: Great AI narrative and strong growth, but the stock is already trading above the average target.
- `CSCO`: Cleaner enterprise / networking than in prior years, but less explosive than `ANET`.
- `DECK`: Good consumer execution and better valuation than many momentum names.
- `DELL`: AI-server leverage is real, but the stock has already run a long way.
- `FCX`: Good copper torque if power and data-center capex stays hot, but still a commodity trade.
- `HPE`: Same broad theme as Dell and networking, but less clean than the top picks.
- `IRM`: Attractive digital-infrastructure angle, though the earnings profile is lower quality than `EQIX`.
- `JBL`: Operationally strong manufacturing / hardware enabler, but a lot is already priced in.
- `KLAC`: Excellent semi-equipment name; I would rather buy a reset than chase a near-vertical move.
- `LRCX`: Same view as `KLAC`; great business, not my favorite entry point.
- `LVS`: China / Macau consumer recovery trade is interesting, but it is not one of my highest-conviction themes.
- `MAR`: High-quality travel compounder, but not as compelling as the infrastructure names.
- `NXPI`: Reasonable valuation, but the business mix is more cyclical and less obviously accelerating.
- `RL`: Premium consumer name with strong execution; good watchlist candidate outside tech.
- `ROST`: Strong operator, but I prefer `DECK` and `RL` on this scan.
- `TEL`: High-quality connector exposure, but `APH` is the better version right now.
- `TMO`: Best-in-class life-science franchise, though the growth re-acceleration is not yet as clear as the core names.
- `WELL`: Good demographic / senior-housing tailwind, but not a first-choice momentum name.
- `WST`: Strong healthcare franchise, but the setup is more of a patient quality watch than an active leadership trade.

## What I Am Not Prioritizing

- I am avoiding the storage winners `SNDK`, `STX`, and `WDC` as top watchlist names even though the momentum is huge. The cycle looks too late and the average analyst targets sit well below price.
- I am not promoting `INTC`, `ON`, `SWKS`, or `MCHP` because I do not see the same clean mix of growth plus quality that I get in `ANET`, `AVGO`, `APH`, or `ETN`.
- I am passing on most regional banks and traditional financials from this scan (`BK`, `CFG`, `FITB`, `KEY`, `TFC`, `STT`, `NTRS`, `GS`, `MS`, `EG`, `GL`, `L`) because the momentum is fine, but the narrative edge is weaker.
- I am also passing on most heavy-cyclical breakout names (`CAT`, `CMI`, `CSX`, `NUE`, `STLD`, `JBHT`, `ROK`) because the reward looks more dependent on macro persistence than on durable business re-rating.

## Process Note

Late-April and May earnings are close for a large part of this universe. I would not force new positions immediately before those reports. For the core names above, the better process is:

1. keep them on the active watchlist now
2. let earnings clear
3. then decide based on guidance, backlog, margins, and reaction quality
